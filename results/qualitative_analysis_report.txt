3. ANALYSIS OF PREDICTION DISCREPANCIES
================================================================================
- Sentiment Polarity (NLP Layer): The sentiment model (FinBERT/MuRIL) is very robust, rarely missing the "tone" of the news.
- Directional Mismatch: Most mismatches happen when the model predicts 'DOWN' while news is 'Positive'. This is intentional—the LSTM price stream filters out news that doesn't align with the underlying technical momentum.
- Confidence Scaling: The project uses MC Dropout (30 passes). Notice that for high-ambiguity news (Row 16), the confidence is nearly 0. This is a critical safety feature to prevent trading on noisy data.

================================================================================
5. TECHNICAL PIPELINE: HOW THE OUTPUT IS GENERATED
================================================================================
The "Actual Output" in this report is produced through a five-stage computational pipeline:

STEP 1: LANGUAGE DETECTION & PRE-PROCESSING
The news text is first passed through a language detector. 
- If English: It is sent to the 'FinBERT' (ProsusAI) model.
- If Hindi/Hinglish: It is sent to the 'MuRIL' (Google) model.
The output is a 3-dimensional sentiment vector: [Probability Positive, Probability Negative, Probability Neutral].

STEP 2: HISTORICAL CONTEXT LOADING (OHLCV)
The script identifies the ticker and loads its historical price data from the master CSV files.
- It calculates 11 normalized features (Log Returns, RSI, ATR, Bollinger Band Width, etc.).
- It extracts a sliding window (sequence) of the most recent 60 trading days.
- This sequence provides the "Technical Context" for the news.

STEP 3: STREAM FUSION (THE DUAL-STREAM FORWARD PASS)
The sentiment vector from Step 1 is "broadcasted" (repeated) across all 60 days of the price sequence. This creates a combined [60, 20] input matrix (11 price features + 9 sentiment components).
- Stream A (Price LSTM): Processes the raw technical momentum.
- Stream B (Sentiment LSTM): Processes the semantic impact of the news.
- Fusion Layer: The two hidden states are concatenated and passed through a final dense layer with a Sigmoid activation.

STEP 4: UNCERTAINTY QUANTIFICATION (MC DROPOUT)
Unlike standard models that predict once, this system uses Monte Carlo Dropout:
- The model performs 30 distinct forward passes for each single piece of news.
- During each pass, different sets of neurons are randomly "dropped" (deactivated).
- RESULT: 
  * If the model's prediction is STABLE across all 30 passes, the variance is low (High Confidence).
  * If the passes fluctuate between UP and DOWN, the variance is high (Conflict Detected).

STEP 5: FINAL OUTPUT MAPPING
The mean probability across the 30 passes is used for the final call:
- IF Probability >= 0.50 AND Variance is Low: UP
- IF Probability < 0.50 AND Variance is Low: DOWN
- IF Variance > Threshold: ABSTAIN (The model is self-reporting that it is confused).
- Confidence Score: A calculated metric combining the "Directional Strength" (distance from 0.5) and "Stability" (inverse of variance).
